
## Project Identity

**Owner:** Albert Kogan. A generalist who moves frequently, travels slowly, and picks up new interests wherever he ends up. Curious by default. Not trying to sell anything — just documenting and sharing what he does.

**Site purpose:** A personal site that collects projects, photos, and occasional writing. It should give any stranger — whether a potential collaborator, someone who got a link, or just a curious person — an honest read on who this is. The site is not a portfolio, not a blog, not a brand. It's closer to: here's my stuff, reach out if you want.

**Content types:**
- Projects and experiments (showing what he builds, not pitching it)
- Photo stories grouped by trip or place, with brief context
- Occasional short notes or observations from life on the move

## Tone and Voice

- Direct, unhurried, slightly warm
- Trusts the reader — never explains what should be obvious
- Confident without performing confidence
- Allowed: dry humor, understatement, small surprises
- Not allowed: buzzwords, superlatives, "personal brand" language, performative vulnerability, declarations about what the site *isn't*

## Design Direction

- Calm, minimal, spacious. Dark tones. The emotional register is a quiet room at night with a good view — use this as a reference point, not a literal brief.
- Design should recede. Content leads. Typography, spacing, and restraint do the work.
- Hidden details and small interactions are welcome for people who like to look closely.
- Nothing should feel over-designed or draw attention to itself.

## Boundaries

- Personal details stay respectful to the people around the owner
- No metrics, follower counts, or social proof
- Professional enough for a stranger, human enough to feel real

## Avoid

- Generic indie-web clichés ("my corner of the internet," "work in progress," "digital garden")
- Anything that sounds like a LinkedIn post or startup pitch
- Over-explaining or selling
- Telling the reader what to feel or what the site is trying to be
